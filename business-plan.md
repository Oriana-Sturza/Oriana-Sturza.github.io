**Date:** May 2026
**Version:** 1.2

## 1. Executive Summary

**Plumir** is a UK-based online marketplace where independent sellers open their own storefront and sell directly to buyers. Unlike commission-based platforms such as Etsy or eBay, Plumir charges sellers a single flat monthly membership fee of **£15.99/month**. Sellers keep 100% of their sale proceeds with no listing limits, no commission, and no setup fees.

The business is a **50/50 partnership**, built on Sharetribe with payments via Stripe, membership automation via Zapier, and customer contact via Tally forms. The immediate focus is completing final pre-launch testing. Marketing has already begun pre-launch across five social media channels to build awareness before going live.

## 4. Market Analysis

### Target Market

**Sellers:** Independent UK artisans, crafters, makers, vintage resellers, and small product businesses — especially those frustrated with unpredictable fees on Etsy or eBay.

**Buyers:** UK consumers who prefer buying from independent sellers and want unique, handmade, or niche products not found in mainstream retail.

### Market Size

Etsy has over 90,000 active UK sellers. Even attracting 0.5–1% of them represents 450–900 potential Plumir sellers — enough to build a profitable platform.

### Competitive Landscape

| Platform | Fee model | Plumir advantage |
|---|---|---|
| Etsy | Listing fee + 6.5% transaction + payment fee | No commission, no listing fees |
| eBay | 12.8%+ of every sale | Predictable flat fee |
| Amazon Handmade | 15% referral fee | No commission |
| Folksy (UK) | Listing fee + 6% commission | No commission |
| Not On The High Street | 25% commission | No commission |

**Core differentiator:** Plumir is the only major UK marketplace with a flat monthly fee and zero commission — sellers always know exactly what they will pay.

### Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Not enough sellers joining at first | Spread the word through social media and UK seller communities; the low fee makes it easy to take a chance |
| Not enough buyers if seller numbers are low | Focus on sellers first — they bring their own existing customers with them |
| Too dependent on Sharetribe as a platform | Monitor Sharetribe's pricing and terms; keep processes documented so the business could move platforms if needed |
| Running at a loss before break-even | Both partners cover the ~£300/month costs until 19+ sellers are active |
| Competing against large established platforms | Compete on pricing fairness, not features — target sellers frustrated with unpredictable fees |

| Area | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Business structure** | 50/50 Partnership. Consider LLP or Ltd as the business grows for liability protection. |
| **UTR numbers** | Both partners have personal UTR numbers. A business UTR for the Plumir partnership will be obtained on registration with HMRC. |
| **HMRC registration** | Register the partnership with HMRC before or at launch. Each partner declares their 50% profit share on their personal Self Assessment (SA100 + SA104). Partnership files SA800. |

## 10. Future Development Roadmap

All items below require **Sharetribe Flex** — the developer/API version of the platform that allows custom coding. Flex costs more than the current plan. Migration happens only once the business is stable and generating consistent revenue.

### 1 — Multi-Item Basket *(first priority)*
Currently one item per transaction. Build a basket so buyers can add items from multiple sellers and check out in one go. Requires custom transaction flow development on Flex.

### 2 — Seller Analytics Dashboard
Sales trends, most viewed listings, conversion data — visible to sellers in their dashboard. Scope depends on what Sharetribe Flex's API exposes; some data may come from Stripe.

- **Starter:** lower price, limited listing slots — low-risk entry for new sellers, upgrade funnel once they see results
- **Premium:** higher price, unlimited listings + extras (promoted listings, analytics, priority support)

### 4 — Mobile App *(Year 2)*
iOS and Android app built against the Flex API. Buyer shopping experience first, then seller store management.

### Additional

- Buyer features — wishlist, seller following, reviews

### Sharetribe Console Instructions

**Categories in alphabetical order**
No automatic sorting exists. Assign a number to each category using metadata — Sharetribe displays them in that order. Set numbers to match alphabetical sequence (Art Supplies = 1, Books = 2, etc.). No coding needed.
Reference: https://www.sharetribe.com/help/en/articles/14434329-how-metadata-works#h_f5d4c3cc13

---

**Search and how buyers find listings**
No tags system — search scans listing titles, descriptions, and text fields. A buyer searching "ceramic mug" will find listings that include those words. Tell sellers in onboarding to write detailed, keyword-rich titles and descriptions.
Reference: https://www.sharetribe.com/help/en/articles/8413312-how-search-works

---

**Age verification for restricted products**
No proper ID verification without coding (Flex only). Best no-code option: add an "I confirm I am 18+" checkbox or date of birth field at sign-up, plus a disclaimer on restricted listings. Not legally watertight — revisit properly on Flex.
To do: Console > Users or Listings settings > add custom field.

---

**Back to top button on policy pages**
Use an anchor link — add a button at the bottom of each policy page and set its link to `#top`. No coding needed — done in the Sharetribe page editor.

---

**Notifying users when a policy changes**
Bulk email not possible natively. Use Mailchimp:
1. Console > Manage > Users > Export CSV
2. Import CSV into Mailchimp (free up to 500 contacts)
3. Send email from Mailchimp
4. Re-export each time to capture new users

Set up a free Mailchimp account now so it is ready.

---

**Seller policies and shipping/returns on their profile**
Can't attach documents without coding. Workaround: add a custom user field where sellers paste a link to a Google Doc with their policies. Buyers click the link to view it.
To do: Console > add user field (e.g. "Shop Policies & Returns — paste your link here"). Add setup instructions to seller onboarding.

---

**How many items a buyer can buy at once from one store**
Test this during the buyer workflow. Sellers should not be able to have multiple items purchased in a single transaction on the current no-code setup — this is the first planned development on Flex.
